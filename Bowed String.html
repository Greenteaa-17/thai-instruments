String Instrument

Source: Click Here!

In Thai music,Bowed string instruments (เครื่องสีไทย – Khrueang See Thai) are instruments that produce sound from vibrating strings, either by plucking, bowing, or striking. They are mainly used in ensembles like the Khrueang See (วงเครื่องสี).

Saw duang (ซอด้วง)

  • High-pitched, bright sound.
  • Two-string fiddle with a small cylindrical body, often covered with snake skin.
  • Played with a bow between the strings.

Saw u (ซออู้)

  • Lower-pitched than saw duang.
  • Larger resonator made from coconut shell, also covered with cow or snake skin.
  • Produces a mellow, deep sound.

Saw sam sai (ซอสามสาย)

  • Rare and prestigious instrument.
  • Three-string fiddle, elaborately decorated.
  • Used in classical court music, producing a soft and elegant tone.

Key Features of Thai Bowed Instruments

  1. Use a bow made from a thin wooden stick strung with horsehair
  2. Playing method: fingers press the strings while the bow is drawn across them
  3. Produce long, continuous sounds, unlike plucked instruments
  4. Commonly used in almost all types of Thai classical music, especially for songs with vocals and in Phleng Thao (elaborated classical pieces)
