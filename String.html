Thai String Instruments (เครื่องสายไทย – Khrueang Sai Thai) Thai string instruments are instruments that produce sound from vibrating strings. The sound can be made by plucking, bowing, or striking. They are used in ensembles such as Khrueang Sai (เครื่องสาย) and Mahori (มโหรี).

Main Types of Thai String Instruments

Jakhe (จะเข้)

  • Crocodile-shaped zither with three strings
  • Played by plucking with a plectrum
  • Produces a deep, soft, and resonant tone

Khim (ขิม)

  • Hammered dulcimer, trapezoid-shaped
  • Strings struck with two small bamboo mallets
  • Produces a bright, sparkling sound

Phin (พิณ) (influenced from Isan/Lao culture)

  • Lute-like instrument with 2–4 strings
  • Plucked, produces lively tones

Characteristics of Thai String Instruments

  1. Versatile: can play melody, harmony, or accompaniment
  2. Important in ensembles: especially in Khrueang Sai, which is softer and more lyrical than the energetic Piphat ensemble
